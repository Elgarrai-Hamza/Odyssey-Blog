Marrakech

Between East and West, Marrakech is in the era of time. It will offer you a total change of scenery just a few hours from Europe while keeping the comfort of modernity. A major attractive city, it enjoys worldwide access thanks to its international airport.

see more

Casablanca

Casablanca the largest city in Morocco. Economic capital, it is located on the Atlantic coast about 80 km south of the administrative capital of the country, Rabat. Third tourist city of the Country, Casablanca tells you about its colonial past through a part of its architecture remained intact.

see more

The Sahara

With silence so intense that you can hear your heartbeat as it rushes blood through your veins, you've come to the perfect place for a peaceful retreat. The minute you step foot on the soft golden dunes of the Sahara Desert, you'll be transported to another world, another time.

see more

Chefchaouen

Chefchaouen is a small town perched in the mountains of northeastern Morocco offering a most magical Mediterranean setting. It is a place with large mythical whitewashed blue corridors and an authentic and welcoming atmosphere.After spending 2 days there, I conclude that it is my favorite city.

see more

Ouarzazate

Ouarzazate, or the "gateway to the desert", has always been a crossroads city between the Sahara and the Atlas regions further north. It is still the same today in terms of tourism, as it is not also used as a base camp for those who wish to explore the surroundings ( Draa Valley , Dades Gorges ...).

see more

Essaouira

Relaxed beach vibes, narrow cobblestone streets of white and blue, vagabond street performers, and some of the tastiest, fresh-off-the-boat seafood you can find. A relaxed sea-side town with a unique Santorini-esque atmosphere, Essaouria is a perfect hidden gem to round out your itinerary

see more

Fes

Trace the roots of Moroccan craftsmanship among the ancient monuments and weavings streets of the Medina. In Fes you'll discover the foundations of Moroccan civilization and culture.

see more

Rabat

Morocco's capital city, Rabat is largely untouched by tourism and a must-see for history enthusiasts keen on tracing Morocco's footsteps through the ages.

see more

Tangier

Tangier, Europes's Gateway to Africa. Nestled in the northeast corner of Morocco, the bustling city of Tangier has long been a haven for artists, writers, rock stars, and intellectuals.
